# Mūla — your values, on your side

*mūla (मूल) — Sanskrit: root.*

Every app you use profiles you. The feed profiles you to hold your attention, the store to move your wallet — all of it optimized for engagement, none of it for you. **Mūla is the other thing: an AI that learns what you actually value, writes it into cards you keep, and works for no one but you.**

We call the underlying idea **meaning-maxxing** — building AI that maximizes meaning instead of engagement or productivity. Attention is what you spend; meaning is what you're spending it *for*. An agent that knows your values — really knows them, in your own words, with your endorsement on every card — can be on your side in a way no engagement-optimized system structurally can.

## What exists

- **The wallet.** A ~30-minute conversation (or a 90-second card swipe, your choice of depth) becomes 3–6 *value cards*: specific, arguable statements of what you pay attention to when you choose. Not horoscopes — each card is concrete enough to disagree with. You endorse, edit, or toss every card; tossed means deleted, actually deleted.
- **The receipts.** Cards are encrypted at rest (ciphertext even to us), exportable and deletable in one tap, and never touched by ads, tokens, or resale. The consent flow is four separate, versioned, logged layers — nothing bundled. See the whitepaper for the full architecture.
- **The method.** Card elicitation builds on the Meaning Alignment Institute's published Moral Graph Elicitation research — values as attentional policies anchored in real choices, not survey answers.

## How we're building it

**In public, with pre-registered honesty.** The company itself runs on an open-source agent stack (compound business — its own repo and story). Decisions, mistakes, and design docs become public as we go. And there's one sealed envelope: we've pre-registered an experiment about where this goes next — hypothesis, methods, and the numbers that would kill it, hash-stamped before we started (`ddbe9cd`). You'll get the results either way, pass or fail.
